Famous Players

Daniel Negreanu

"Kid Poker"

Negreanu is a professional poker player from Ontario who has won six World Series of Poker (WSOP) bracelets, two World Poker Tour championship titles and has reached numerous final tables. Since 2004 he has been named "Player of the Year" several times and was also awarded with the title of "Favorite Poker Player" in 2006. In 2014 he was inducted into the Poker Hall of Fame in Las Vegas. Negreanu explained that the most important skill he employs is observing what hands his opponents play and how capable they are of playing them. As of 2015, his total live tournament winnings exceed $29,825,000.

Phil Hellmuth

"The Poker Brat"

Aside from Hellmuth's unpredictable "poker brat" personality, this Californian poker player has had a successful poker career. He has acquired thirteen WSOP bracelets, won the Main Event of the 1989 WSOP, as well as the Main Event of the 2012 World Series of Poker Europe. He accompanies Daniel Negreanu as a member of the Poker Hall of Fame. Hellmuth shares his knowledge with the public by making instructional poker videos and writing a number of books. Dropping out of college to play poker ended up working out for Hellmuth as his total live tournament winnings amount to $18,175,409.

Phillip Ivey

"The Tiger Woods of Poker"

Numerous poker observers and contemporaries consider Ivey the best all-round player in the world today. He has won ten WSOP bracelets, one World Poker Tour title, and has appeared at nine World Poker Tour final tables. At age 38, he is the youngest player to ever win ten WSOP bracelets. In 2014 he lost an attempted lawsuit against a British casino he accused of witholding his $12.4 million winnings. The casino convinced the court that Ivey kept track of card values by watching for design imperfections on the backs of the cards, and so they did not end up paying him anything. As of 2014, Ivey's total live tournament winnings exceed $21,400,000. He has also won millions playing online and in cash games.
